# International Student Mental Health Analysis

## Project Overview
This project explores whether studying in a foreign country affects student mental health. 
Using survey data collected from a Japanese international university in 2018, the analysis investigates the relationship between depression, social connectedness, acculturative stress, and length of stay among international students.

The analysis was performed using PostgreSQL.

## Research Questions
- Do international students show higher levels of depression than domestic students?
- Does the length of stay affect depression levels among international students?
- Are acculturative stress and social connectedness related to depression?

## Tools Used
- SQL
- PostgreSQL
- Data analysis

## Dataset
The dataset contains survey responses from students including:
- depression score
- acculturative stress score
- social connectedness score
- length of stay
- student type (international or domestic)

## Key Findings
- International students showed higher average depression scores than domestic students.
- Acculturative stress was positively correlated with depression.
- Students with shorter stays tended to show higher stress levels.

## Conclusion
The analysis supports previous research findings that international students face increased mental health challenges, particularly due to cultural adjustment and social integration.
